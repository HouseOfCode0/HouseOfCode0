About Me

The still is from the movie "The Social Network," where Eduardo pens down the Elo algorithm used to rate chess players. I was fascinated by how a few lines of algebraic expressions could be transformed into a precise problem-solving instruction set. I still remember this movie as my first source of inspiration in the world of technology. It was my initial introduction to the fast-paced world of startups, entrepreneurship, and technology. It motivated me and made me think about the impact that a small group of individuals can have on the world by building something groundbreaking and revolutionizing the world around us. Driven by this curiosity, I was propelled into the world of software strategy, creation, and change.

Projects

Gene Networks

In my opinion, most medical treatments improved the symptoms and not the root cause of the disease. Also, I believe modern medicine lacks a clear understanding of many physiological aspects of the human body. These unsolved mysteries of the human body intrigued me. Following my curiosity and passion, I took up the role of a Bioinformatician and machine learning engineer at Merck. My research initiative focuses on decoding the complexities of neurodegenerative diseases, including Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, and ALS, using advanced machine-learning techniques. By delving into gene regulatory networks, my aim is to uncover hidden patterns and essential regulatory hubs that play a pivotal role in the development of these debilitating conditions.

Tensorflow

This repository is a collection of projects focused on the ever-growing field of machine learning. It features a diverse range of projects, aimed at exploring different areas of this field, from object identification, transfer learning, natural language processing, and sentiment analysis, to name a few. These projects leverage a variety of neural network models, including convolutional neural networks (CNNs), recurrent neural networks (RNNs), long short-term memory (LSTM) networks, and gated recurrent unit (GRU) networks. By working on these different projects, you can expect to gain a comprehensive understanding of the inner workings of these networks and the principles behind them. One particularly exciting project in development is a cycleGAN that can generate different beards and hairstyles on user images. This project is a testament to the versatility of machine learning and the potential to create innovative and creative solutions to everyday problems.
